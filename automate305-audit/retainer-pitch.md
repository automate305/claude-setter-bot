# AUTOMATE305 — Retainer Pitch Script

## When to Use This

After you've delivered the audit report. This is the transition from "free value" to "paid engagement." The report does the heavy lifting. This script closes.

---

## The Core Pitch Philosophy

1. **The report is the proof.** We already showed we know their business. Don't re-explain — refer back.
2. **Make it specific.** Generic pitches die. Every pitch references their actual gaps.
3. **The retainer is a no-brainer if the audit score is under 70.** Frame it that way.
4. **Price is never the first thing you say.** Value always comes before price.

---

## Phase 1 — Audit Debrief (3-5 min)

After sending or presenting the report:

> "So that's what we found. Did anything surprise you, or does this match what you've been feeling?"

[Let them talk. Listen for:]
- Which gap stings the most (that's your anchor)
- Whether they've tried to fix it before
- Their emotional reaction (frustrated = motivated to change)

> "What you scored [X]/100 on — the [Pillar they scored lowest] — that's actually the most common gap we see with [their industry]. The good news is it's also the most fixable."

---

## Phase 2 — Paint the Picture (2-3 min)

Before you pitch anything, paint the after-state:

> "Imagine six months from now — your website is converting, your social is working on a schedule you never have to think about, leads are being followed up automatically, and you're getting a monthly report telling you exactly what's happening. That's what we build."

> "Most of our clients tell us the same thing after month two: 'I can't believe I was doing all of this manually.'"

Specific example (use one that fits their industry):

**For local businesses (med spas, law firms, restaurants):**
> "We worked with a [similar business] in [area]. They were at about a [similar score]. Three months later they had a full lead gen system, 800 people on their email list, and their Instagram grew from [X] to [Y]. All automated."

**For coaches/consultants:**
> "The biggest thing we see with coaches is they're relying 100% on word of mouth and Instagram, and when posting slows down, leads slow down. We fix that by building systems that work even when you're offline."

**For e-commerce:**
> "You're leaving money on the table every week with no email follow-up. On average, email generates $42 for every $1 spent. That's not theoretical — that's what happens when the system is in place."

---

## Phase 3 — The Offer (2 min)

> "So based on what we found, here's what I'd recommend."

[Pull up the recommended tier from the report]

> "The [Tier Name] at $[price]/month. What that gives you is: [list 3 specific deliverables that directly address their biggest gaps]."

> "We'd start with [specific Month 1 deliverable], which directly fixes the [their lowest scoring pillar] issue we found. That alone would move your score from [X] to around [Y]."

> "And unlike an agency that just posts content and calls it a day — everything we build is automated, so it keeps running whether you're thinking about marketing or not."

---

## Phase 4 — Handle the Price Moment

They will pause. That's normal. Here's how to handle the most common responses:

### "That's a lot / I need to think about it"
> "Totally fair. What specifically feels like the biggest hurdle — is it the timing, the investment, or you're not sure if this is the right fit?"

[Listen. Then address the specific thing, not the general hesitation]

> "Here's what I'd say: the gaps we found are costing you money every month. Every month you wait is a month of leads that aren't being captured, content that isn't running, and follow-ups that aren't happening. We can't make that decision for you, but I want to make sure you're not leaving money on the table just because it felt like a big step."

### "Can we start smaller?"
> "Yes. The Starter plan at $1,500 is built specifically for that. We'd focus entirely on [their #1 gap] and build from there. Most clients move up to Growth within 60-90 days once they see how it works."

### "We're doing some of this already"
> "Good. What we bring is the AI layer and the integration — everything working together versus tools that don't talk to each other. Walk me through what you've got set up and I'll tell you exactly what we'd add or replace."

### "We tried an agency before and it didn't work"
> "What happened? I want to understand what went wrong before I tell you we're different."

[Listen to the story. Then:]

> "That's actually a common failure pattern. Most agencies do outputs — posts, emails, content. We do systems. If the system isn't built, the outputs stop the second you stop paying. We build it so it keeps running."

### "I need to check with my partner / spouse"
> "Of course. If it helps, I can send a quick summary of the report and the recommended plan so they can review it too. When do you think you'd have that conversation?"

[Get a specific follow-up date]

---

## Phase 5 — Close or Next Step

**If they're ready:**
> "Great. Here's what happens next: I'll send over the agreement and a kickoff questionnaire. We'll schedule a 60-min onboarding call and hit the ground running. First deliverable lands in week two."

**If they need a follow-up:**
> "I'll send you the full report in an email so you have everything in one place. I'll follow up [specific day] — does [time] work for a quick check-in?"

**If it's a no for now:**
> "Totally understand. I'll send the report — even if timing isn't right, the gaps are real and you should fix them one way or another. If anything changes or you want to revisit, just reach out."

## Objection Cheat Sheet

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|----------|
| Too expensive | "What's your current cost of leads? Let's do the math." |
| Not the right time | "What would make it the right time? Let's work backward from that." |
| We do it in-house | "Great. We can audit and optimize what you have, or fill the gaps your team doesn't cover." |
| We get clients by referral | "Referrals dry up. Systems don't. This protects your pipeline." |
| I don't believe in [social/email/etc] | "Fair. What IS working for you right now? Let's build on that." |
| Can I see examples? | "Yes. [Direct to case study or example — have these ready]" |

---

## The One-Liner Close

If they're on the fence and you need a final nudge:

> "Here's the simplest way I can put it: you spent [time] with us today. The audit showed [X gaps]. Either you fix them yourself, hire someone to do it piecemeal, or let us build the whole system for you and own it. We're the fastest path from where you are to where you want to be."
