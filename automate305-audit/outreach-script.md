# AUTOMATE305 — Outreach Script
## Getting Strangers to Say YES to the Free Audit

---

## The Core Philosophy

Most agencies say: *"Hey, can I audit you for free?"*
That sounds like: *"Hey, can I pitch you something?"*

AUTOMATE305 says: **"I already looked. Here's one thing I noticed. Want the full picture?"**

That sounds like: *"This person actually knows their stuff and already found something real."*

The difference is specificity. Generic outreach dies. Observation-led outreach converts.

**The Formula:**
1. One specific observation about their business (good + gap)
2. Short, low-commitment offer ("quick 20-min audit")
3. Remove the catch upfront ("you walk away with the findings either way")

Never lead with what you do. Lead with what you noticed about THEM.

---

## Step 0 — Pre-Outreach Qualification (60 Seconds Per Prospect)

Do NOT send any outreach until you've answered YES to all of these:

- [ ] **Real business?** — Real product/service, actual customers, not a hobby project
- [ ] **Digital presence?** — Website + at least one active social account
- [ ] **Right geo?** — Miami/South FL primary, US secondary, no international
- [ ] **Revenue signal?** — Appears to be doing $10K+/mo (ads running, professional photos, team, testimonials)
- [ ] **Obvious gap?** — You can see at least ONE specific problem in under 60 seconds
- [ ] **Not locked up?** — No signs of strong agency relationship (consistent high-quality content with no cracks)
- [ ] **Right ICP?** — Service business, local business, coach, e-commerce, or creator-adjacent

**If any box is unchecked → skip.** Don't audit bad fits. Time is the asset.

**Prospect score (quick gut check):**
- 5-7 obvious gaps visible = Priority outreach
- 2-4 gaps visible = Standard outreach
- 0-1 gaps visible = Pass or plant a seed only

## The Observation Bank (Find ONE Before Messaging)

Before touching any outreach template, spend 60 seconds finding the specific observation.
Pick the most obvious gap from this list:

**Website observations:**
- "Your homepage headline doesn't tell me what you do in 5 seconds"
- "No CTA above the fold on mobile"
- "No social proof visible without scrolling"
- "Contact form but no email capture / lead magnet"

**Instagram observations:**
- "Great content but bio isn't converting traffic to anything"
- "Posting consistently but every post ends with no CTA"
- "No Reels — algorithm is pushing you down compared to competitors"
- "Engagement is solid but no funnel behind it"
- "9-grid looks inconsistent — brand feels scattered"
- "Bio is all about you, not about what the client gets"

**Lead gen observations:**
- "No lead magnet — you're sending people to a homepage with no capture"
- "Running ads but landing page doesn't match the ad message"
- "Strong following but no email list visible"

**Automation observations:**
- "Booking via DM back-and-forth — Calendly would fix that immediately"
- "No automated follow-up sequence visible"

**Pick ONE. That's your opener.**

## Platform Scripts

---

### INSTAGRAM DM — Primary Platform

**Rules for IG DMs:**
- 3 sentences max on the opening message
- Never use "synergy," "leverage," "scale," or "optimize"
- Sound like a person, not an agency
- No logo, no link, no pitch in message 1

---

**Template A — The Observation Opener (Best performer)**

> "Hey [Name] — came across your page and love what you're building. Quick thing I noticed: [one specific observation]. Would it be helpful if I put together a free 5-point audit of your whole digital presence? Takes me 20 min, you get the full breakdown regardless of what you do with it."

**Example:**
> "Hey Maria — came across your page and love what you're doing with the med spa content. Quick thing I noticed: your bio has great energy but there's no CTA or link directing people anywhere. Would it be helpful if I put together a free audit of your whole digital presence? Takes me 20 min, you get the full breakdown regardless of what you do with it."

---

**Template B — The Dropped Insight (Value-first)**

> "Hey [Name] — was looking at your [website/IG] and had a thought. [One-sentence insight that's actually useful.] Happy to put together a quick free audit if you want the full picture — no strings attached."

**Example:**
> "Hey Carlos — was looking at your site and had a thought. Your services page is strong but your homepage doesn't tell me you're Miami-based until the footer — and local SEO is half your opportunity. Happy to put together a quick free audit if you want the full picture — no strings attached."

**Or Spanglish (very Miami, very natural):**

## The 3-Touch Follow-Up Sequence

Most prospects don't respond to message 1. That's normal. Follow up twice more.

### Touch 1 — Initial Outreach
[Use templates above]
Send and wait 48-72 hours.

---

### Touch 2 — The Value Add (48-72 hours later, if no response)

Don't resend the same message. Drop more value or reframe.

**Option A — Drop a new insight:**
> "Hey — didn't want to leave this in your DMs without following up. Also noticed [second observation — different from the first]. Two things worth fixing. Free audit offer still stands if you want the full picture."

**Option B — Social proof reframe:**
> "Hey [Name] — just wrapped an audit for a [similar industry] in Miami this week. Found 4 gaps costing them an estimated [X leads/mo or $X]. Offer stands if you want me to do the same for [their business]."

**Option C — Direct re-ask:**
> "Hey — circling back on the free audit. Totally understand if timing's off. If it's useful down the line, just let me know."

---

### Touch 3 — The Breakup (5-7 days after touch 2, if still no response)

Low pressure. Opens the door without pushing.

> "Hey [Name] — I'll leave you alone after this one. The audit offer stands whenever timing is right. If you ever want a fresh set of eyes on your [website/social/funnel], just drop me a message. Good luck with [specific thing you noticed about their business — shows you actually paid attention]."

**Why this works:** It's the least pushy message in the sequence and often the one that gets a reply. People respond when the pressure disappears.

## Reply Handling — Every Response Type

### "Sure, sounds good / I'm interested"

> "Perfect. I'll do the analysis this week — takes me about 20 minutes on my end. Can I grab your website URL and your Instagram handle to make sure I'm looking at everything? I'll come back with the full breakdown."

[Run the audit. Fill the report template. Book a 30-min debrief call.]

---

### "How much does it cost?"

> "The audit is completely free — no invoice, no strings. If you love what we put together and want help implementing it, we can talk about that separately. But you walk away with the full analysis either way."

---

### "What's the catch?"

> "Honestly? We do this because the audit itself shows you what we're capable of. If it's good, you might want to work with us. If it's not, you still get a free analysis of your business. We've done enough of these that we're confident in the work."

---

### "I already have someone handling my marketing"

> "That's great — the audit might actually be useful as a second opinion. And if everything's solid, I'll tell you that too. No agenda."

---

### "I'm too busy right now"

> "Totally get it. The audit only takes 20 minutes on your end — I do all the analysis beforehand and just walk you through it. But if timing's off, I can check back in [timeframe]. When would be better?"

---

### "Not interested"

> "No worries at all — appreciate you getting back to me. Good luck with [business]."

[Move to disqualified in prospect-tracker.md. Do not follow up again.]

---

### "Tell me more about what you do"

The free audit is how we usually start because it shows exactly where the gaps are before recommending anything. Want me to pull it together for [their business]?"

### No response after Touch 3

Move to `prospect-tracker.md` as "No Response — Cold."
Set a 90-day re-engage date. Things change.

## The Booking Bridge (Once They Say Yes)

Once they agree to the audit, lock it in immediately. Don't let it go cold.

**Step 1 — Confirm what you need:**
> "Perfect. To make sure the audit is actually useful, can you share: (1) your website URL, (2) your Instagram handle, and (3) the one thing in your marketing that's been the biggest headache lately?"

**Step 2 — Set the timeline:**
> "I'll have the analysis ready within 48 hours. Then let's do a quick 20-30 min call to walk through it — what does your schedule look like [day] or [day]?"

**Step 3 — Send the Calendly:**
> "Here's my link — grab whatever time works: [Calendly URL]"

**Step 4 — Confirmation message (after they book):**
> "Locked in — see you [day/time]. I'll have the full breakdown ready. Anything specific you want me to pay extra attention to?"

## Miami Outreach Calendar — High-Leverage Windows

These are the best times to run outreach in Miami. Local businesses are thinking about marketing at these moments:

| Time | Opportunity |
|------|-------------|
| **January** | New year energy. "This is the year we fix our marketing." |
| **April–May** | Pre-summer push. Tourism season ending, locals refocusing. |
| **September** | Post-summer reset. Kids back to school, owners back to growth mode. |
| **October–November** | Pre-holiday sprint. E-commerce and restaurants especially. |
| **After Art Basel (Dec)** | Reflection + planning season. Miami businesses feel the energy. |
| **Post-local events** | After networking events, chamber meetings, BNI — follow up same day. |

**Avoid:** December 15 – January 5 (everyone's checked out), mid-summer July (slower cycle for many).

## Outreach Tracker (Use in prospect-tracker.md)

When logging outreach activity, record:
- Prospect name + handle
- Platform used
- Template used (A/B/C/D/E)
- Date of touch 1, 2, 3
- Response type
- Outcome: Audit booked / No response / Not interested / Follow-up scheduled

Track your conversion rates:
- Outreach sent → response rate: ___% (target: 15-25%)
- Response → audit booked: ___% (target: 40-60%)
- Audit booked → pitch made: ___% (target: 90%+)
- Pitch made → retainer closed: ___% (target: 20-30%)

**If response rate is under 10%:** Observation isn't specific enough. Go back to the Observation Bank and get more targeted.
**If audit → close rate is under 15%:** Report isn't landing. Review pitch script and tighten the "What We'd Do" section.
