# Skill: Competitive Intelligence

## Purpose
Pull a prospect's top 3 competitors, score them across the same 5 pillars, and show the prospect exactly where they stand in their market. This is the audit's "nuclear moment" — when a prospect sees a competitor doing something they're not, the retainer becomes urgent, not just interesting.

**The psychological shift:** Moving from "your marketing could be better" to "your competitor is eating your lunch" triggers loss aversion — the strongest buying motivator there is.

---

## When to Use This

- Include in every audit where competition is clearly relevant (local businesses especially)
- Lead with it in the pitch debrief for maximum impact
- Skip for very niche or truly unique businesses with no direct competitors

---

## Step 1 — Identify the Right Competitors (5 minutes)

Find 3 competitors. Prioritize in this order:

**Tier 1 — Direct local competitors (same city, same service):**
- Google: `[their service] Miami` or `[their service] [their neighborhood]`
- Instagram: search their niche + Miami hashtags
- Yelp, Google Maps (for local service businesses)

**Tier 2 — Aspirational competitors (same service, stronger brand):**
- The business in their niche doing it best in Miami
- The one they probably already know and feel behind

**Tier 3 — National comparison (if relevant):**
- A well-known brand in their space doing marketing right
- Use sparingly — local feels more threatening than national

**Record for each competitor:**
- Business name
- Website URL
- Instagram handle
- Approximate following / engagement
- One thing they're doing noticeably well

---

## Step 2 — Run the 5-Pillar Snapshot (3 minutes per competitor)

Don't do a full audit — do a rapid snapshot. Score each competitor 1-5 per pillar (not 0-20, this is a comparison tool, not a deep audit).

| Pillar | [Prospect] | Competitor 1 | Competitor 2 | Competitor 3 |
|--------|-----------|-------------|-------------|-------------|
| Website & Messaging | /5 | /5 | /5 | /5 |
| Social Presence | /5 | /5 | /5 | /5 |
| Lead Generation | /5 | /5 | /5 | /5 |
| Automation Readiness | /5 | /5 | /5 | /5 |
| AI / Content Systems | /5 | /5 | /5 | /5 |
| **TOTAL** | **/25** | **/25** | **/25** | **/25** |

**Scoring guide (1-5):**
- 5 = Clearly excellent, obvious investment, professional execution
- 4 = Good, above average, some standout elements
- 3 = Average, functional but nothing remarkable
- 2 = Below average, obvious gaps, inconsistent
- 1 = Weak or nonexistent

---

## Step 3 — The Comparison Narrative

After scoring, write 1-2 sentences per competitor for the report. Lead with what the competitor is doing BETTER than the prospect — this is the tension that drives urgency.

**Format:**
> "[Competitor Name] — Score: [X]/25. They're beating [Prospect] on [specific pillar]: [one concrete example of what they're doing better]. Their [specific asset] is particularly strong — [describe it]."

**Example:**
> "Glow Med Spa (their main competitor on Brickell) — Score: 18/25. They're ahead on social and lead gen: posting Reels 5x/week with consistent before/after proof, and they have a lead magnet ('Free Skin Assessment Guide') that feeds into a 6-email welcome sequence. Their IG engagement rate is 6.2% — about 3x what [Prospect] is seeing."

---

## Step 4 — The Gap Analysis (The "Nuclear Moment")

Find the single most impactful gap where a competitor is winning.

**The 3 gap types (in order of urgency):**

**1. Lead capture gap** — Competitor has email capture / lead magnet, prospect doesn't.
> "Every week [Competitor] is capturing leads that came to Google looking for [service] in Miami. Those leads are choosing between you and them — and right now they're landing on [Competitor]'s site, seeing a free offer, opting in, and getting 6 emails before they ever talk to you."

**2. Social system gap** — Competitor is posting consistently with strategy, prospect is sporadic.
> "[Competitor] has posted Reels every week for the last 4 months. Instagram's algorithm is rewarding that consistency with organic reach you're not getting. Right now they're being shown to your potential customers, and you're not."

**3. Visibility gap** — Competitor shows up in search, prospect doesn't.
> "Search '[their service] Miami' right now. [Competitor] is in the top 3. You're on page 2. That's not a product problem — that's a marketing system problem."

---

## Step 5 — The Opportunity Flip

Always balance the competitive threat with an upside. Show the prospect they're not losing — they just haven't started yet.

**The flip:**
> "The good news: [Competitor] is winning on [specific thing] but they have their own gaps. Their [website copy / social engagement / lead funnel] is actually weaker than yours in [specific way]. If we fix your [top gap] first, you close the gap fast — and then you're competing from a position of strength, not catch-up."

**Why this matters:** You don't want the prospect to feel hopeless. You want them feeling urgent AND optimistic. Urgent alone → paralysis. Urgent + optimistic → action.

## Quick Competitive Intel by Industry

Use these as starting points if you're short on time:

### Med Spas / Aesthetics (Miami)
Common winners: Heavy before/after Reels, Google review volume, booking link everywhere
Common gaps in the market: Almost nobody has a proper lead magnet or email sequence
**Prospect's angle:** "Most Miami med spas compete on Instagram aesthetics. The one that builds an email list and follow-up system first wins long-term."

### Law Firms (Miami)
Common winners: Google Ads + strong review presence, clear practice area pages
Common gaps: Almost no one is doing content marketing or email nurture
**Prospect's angle:** "You can't outspend the big firms on ads. But you can out-content them — and most haven't started."

### Real Estate Agents (Miami)
Common winners: Heavy video content, neighborhood expertise content, strong personal brand
Common gaps: Email follow-up for leads, CRM automation, past-client reactivation
**Prospect's angle:** "The top producers aren't posting more — they have systems that keep them top-of-mind without daily effort."

### Restaurants (Miami)
Common winners: Food Reels (extremely high organic reach), Google reviews, local influencer features
Common gaps: Email/SMS list, loyalty program, event marketing automation
**Prospect's angle:** "One viral Reel can fill tables for a week. A loyalty email list fills them every week."

### Coaches / Consultants
Common winners: Consistent educational content, strong testimonial social proof, clear funnel
Common gaps: Automated lead nurture, scalable content system, AI-assisted production
**Prospect's angle:** "The coaches who are winning right now have a content machine, not a content habit. One produces whether they're tired or not. The other stops when life gets busy."

### E-Commerce
Common winners: Email flows (welcome, abandon cart, post-purchase), UGC content, retargeting ads
Common gaps: SMS marketing, review automation, content repurposing system
**Prospect's angle:** "Your competitor is making money from customers you already paid to acquire, through email flows you don't have yet."

```

| | [Prospect] | [Comp 1] | [Comp 2] | [Comp 3] |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Website | /5 | /5 | /5 | /5 |

**Where [Competitor] is ahead:** [1-2 sentences — the gap that creates urgency]

---
```

## The Competitive Pitch Bridge

After presenting the competitive section in the audit debrief:

> "So you can see — you're not losing because your [service/product] is worse. You're losing visibility because the systems aren't in place. [Competitor] isn't smarter or better at what they do. They just have better marketing infrastructure. That's exactly what we build."

**If they say "I didn't know they were doing all that":**
> "Most business owners don't — because you're focused on running your business, not watching competitors. That's actually one of the things we do on retainer: a monthly competitive snapshot so you always know where you stand without having to look yourself."

**This is your upsell moment for the Scale tier** — monthly competitive intelligence becomes a deliverable at $4,500/mo.

---

## Time Budget for This Skill

- Competitor identification: 5 min
- 3 rapid snapshots: 9 min (3 min each)
- Gap analysis + narrative: 5 min
- Total: ~20 minutes

Run this in parallel with the main audit, not after it. By the time you finish the 5-pillar audit on the prospect, you already have the competitive context to make it land harder.
