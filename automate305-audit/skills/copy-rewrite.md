# Skill: Copy Rewrite (Proof of Concept Generator)

## Purpose
During an audit, generate a quick before/after copy sample to include in the report. This is the highest-impact proof of value we can show in the free audit — it makes prospects feel the gap immediately.

Keep it short. One headline, one CTA, one bio. Don't rewrite their whole site. Just enough to make them think "...holy sh*t, that's so much better."

---

## When to Use This

- You've identified a weak headline or CTA during the website audit
- Their Instagram bio is vague or missing a hook
- Their email subject lines are weak (if you can see them)
- They have no clear value proposition

---

## The Rewrite Framework (AIDA Compressed)

Every great short-form copy piece does this in 1-3 lines:
1. **Hook** — Interrupt the scroll or the skim. Specific, unexpected, or contrarian.
2. **Promise** — What the reader gets. Specific outcome, not vague benefit.
3. **CTA** — One action. Active verb. No ambiguity.

## Instagram Bio Rewrites

**Bio formula:** [Who you help] + [Specific result you deliver] + [Social proof or hook] + [CTA with link]

**Examples:**

## Email Subject Line Rewrites

**Formula options:**
- Question: "Are you making this mistake with your [thing]?"
- Number: "3 reasons your [thing] isn't converting"
- Direct: "Your free audit results are in"
- Curiosity: "I found something on your website..."
- Specificity: "How [Client Name] went from X to Y in 60 days"

---

## How to Use This in the Report

1. Find their weakest copy element (headline, bio, or CTA)
2. Write ONE rewrite using the frameworks above
3. Present it in the report under the relevant pillar as:

```
BEFORE: "[Their actual copy]"
AFTER:  "[Your rewrite]"

This one change alone typically improves click-through rate by 15-40%.
```

4. During the pitch call, reference it: "You saw the headline rewrite we included — that took us about 90 seconds. Imagine what we do when we have full access."

---

## Voice Rules for All Copy

Writes for AUTOMATE305 and for clients:
- **Specific beats vague.** "47 clients" > "many clients." "$8K" > "significant results."
- **Second person dominates.** "You" and "your" appear 3x more than "we" and "our."
- **One idea per sentence.** Never combine two benefits in one sentence.
- **Active voice.** "We build systems" not "Systems are built by us."
- **No fluff.** Delete "world-class," "passionate," "dedicated," "innovative."
- **End with action.** Every piece of copy ends with a next step.
