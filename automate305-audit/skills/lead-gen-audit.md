# Skill: Lead Generation Audit

## Purpose
Diagnose how well a prospect captures, nurtures, and converts leads from their existing traffic. Most businesses have more traffic than they realize — and zero system to capture any of it. This audit quantifies the leak and prices it in dollars.

**The core insight to land with every prospect:**
> "You're not getting more leads because you have a traffic problem. You're getting fewer leads than you should because you have a capture problem. Every person who visits your website or follows your Instagram and doesn't give you their contact info is gone forever. We're here to fix the bucket before we worry about filling it."

---

## The 5-Stage Lead Gen System

A complete lead gen system has 5 stages. Identify exactly which stages exist, which are broken, and which are missing entirely.

```
TRAFFIC → [1. CAPTURE] → [2. NURTURE] → [3. CONVERT] → [4. FOLLOW-UP] → [5. RE-ENGAGE]
```

| Stage | What It Is | Most Common Status |
|-------|-----------|-------------------|
| **1. Capture** | Lead magnet + opt-in mechanism | Missing entirely |
| **2. Nurture** | Welcome email sequence | Missing or 1 generic email |
| **3. Convert** | CTA strategy inside emails/content | Missing |
| **4. Follow-Up** | Post-inquiry sales process | Manual, inconsistent |
| **5. Re-Engage** | Dormant leads + past clients reactivated | Never done |

**Every missing stage = recoverable revenue. Map them all.**

## Step 1 — Traffic Baseline (2 minutes)

Before diagnosing the capture problem, estimate the traffic opportunity.

**Website traffic signals (look for these):**
- Any visible analytics widget on site? (Rare but possible)
- Estimated traffic via SEMrush/SimilarWeb if you have access
- Proxy signal: Google review count × 50-100 = rough monthly visitors
- Social following ÷ 10 = rough monthly website clicks from social

**Don't need exact numbers — ballpark is enough for the math.**

Estimate: approximately _____ monthly website visitors
Estimate: approximately _____ Instagram monthly reach (check their Reels view count × 4)

**Why this matters:** You'll use these numbers in Step 7 (The Revenue Math) to show exactly what a working capture system would produce.

---

## Step 2 — Capture Infrastructure Audit

**Email Opt-In Check (website):**
- [ ] Is there ANY email capture on the website?
  - Pop-up / exit intent form
  - Embedded opt-in form (above fold, mid-page, footer)
  - "Join our list" section
  - Gated content (download in exchange for email)
- [ ] If yes: what's the offer? ("Subscribe to our newsletter" = weak. Specific lead magnet = strong)
- [ ] If yes: where does it appear? (Above fold = strong. Footer only = weak)

**Score:**
- Prominent, specific-offer opt-in: +4
- Opt-in exists but generic or buried: +2
- No email capture at all: 0

**Lead Magnet Check:**
- [ ] Do they offer anything free in exchange for contact info?
- [ ] Is the lead magnet clearly named and described?
- [ ] Is it a **high-perceived-value** offer or a generic PDF?

**Lead Magnet Quality Grade (see Step 3 for grading)**

**Social Lead Capture:**
- [ ] Instagram: Does their bio CTA lead to a lead capture page or just a homepage?
- [ ] Do they use "DM me for [X]" calls to action in content? (Soft capture)
- [ ] ManyChat or IG automation active? (Keyword → email capture flow)
- [ ] Link-in-bio: Linktree/Beacons with email opt-in, or just a website link?

**CRM / Contact Tracking:**
- [ ] Is any CRM visible? (Check site footer for "Powered by [CRM]", or ask directly)
- [ ] Do they have a contact form that feeds somewhere?
- [ ] Is there a booking system? (Calendly, Acuity, etc.)

## Step 3 — Lead Magnet Quality Grading

Not all lead magnets convert. Grade theirs honestly. If they don't have one, recommend the right one for their industry (see Industry Lead Magnet Bank below).

**The 5-Tier Grading System:**

| Grade | Description | Conversion Rate |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **A — Irresistible** | Solves one specific, painful problem for exact ICP. Immediate value. Directly connected to paid offer. | 5-15% |
| **B — Solid** | Specific, useful, relevant. Good offer but not perfectly tuned. | 3-7% |
| **C — Average** | Generic checklist or guide. Relevant industry but not specific enough. | 1-3% |
| **D — Weak** | "Subscribe for updates" or vague ebook. No specific promise. | 0.5-1% |
| **F — None** | No lead magnet. Missing the most important capture mechanism. | 0% |

**Grade their lead magnet: ___**

**What makes a lead magnet Grade A:**
1. **Specific outcome** — "Lose 10 lbs" not "get healthy"
2. **Fast to consume** — Quiz, checklist, short video, calculator (not a 50-page ebook)
3. **Immediately useful** — They get value before they even talk to you
4. **Connected to the paid offer** — The free thing is a preview of the paid thing
5. **Named like a product** — "The Miami Med Spa Skin Match Quiz" not "Free Guide"

## Step 4 — Email Sequence Audit

If they have an email platform, dig into what happens after someone opts in.

**Platform Detection:**
Look at any email they've sent (check their newsletter, unsubscribe footer, or ask).
Common platforms and what they signal:

| Platform | Signal |
|----------|--------|
| Mailchimp | Starter level. Basic sequences possible. |
| Klaviyo | E-commerce-focused. Good automation. |
| ActiveCampaign | Automation-forward. Can be powerful if used right. |
| ConvertKit/Kit | Creator/coach oriented. |
| HubSpot | B2B. CRM + email together. |
| Constant Contact | Older, basic. Usually just broadcast emails. |
| **None detected** | Biggest gap possible. |

**Sequence Audit (if they have one):**

Subscribe to their list yourself (use a test email) and record what happens:

| Email # | Sent When | Subject Line | Has CTA? | Grade (A-F) |
|---------|-----------|-------------|----------|-------------|
| 1 | Immediately? | | | |
| 2 | Day ___? | | | |
| 3 | Day ___? | | | |
| 4+ | | | | |

**Benchmarks for a healthy welcome sequence:**
- Email 1: Sent **instantly** (within 5 minutes of opt-in)
- Sequence length: **5-7 emails minimum** over 10-14 days
- Open rate benchmark: 40-60% for email 1, 25-40% for subsequent
- Click rate benchmark: 3-8% per email
- Every email should have **one clear CTA**

**Red flags:**
- Email 1 arrives hours later or not at all
- Only 1-2 emails in the sequence
- No CTA in any email
- Every email is promotional (no education, no trust-building)
- Generic subject lines ("Welcome to our newsletter!")

## Step 5 — Ongoing Email Marketing Check

Beyond the welcome sequence — are they actively marketing to their list?

**Frequency:**
- 1x/week or more: Active, strong (+4)
- 2-3x/month: Acceptable (+2)
- Monthly or less: Weak (+1)
- Never or dormant list: 0

**Content quality indicators (look at their last 3 emails if accessible):**
- [ ] Specific subject lines (not "Monthly Update — April")
- [ ] Personal/conversational tone (not corporate blast)
- [ ] One topic per email (not everything at once)
- [ ] Clear CTA at the bottom
- [ ] Mobile-optimized (single column, large text)

**List health questions (ask the prospect):**
- "How big is your email list right now?"
- "How often are you sending?"
- "What platform are you using?"
- "What's your average open rate?" (if they know)

**Average open rate benchmarks by industry (2025):**
| Industry | Good | Average | Poor |
|----------|------|---------|------|
| Med Spa / Beauty | 30%+ | 20-30% | Under 20% |
| Legal | 25%+ | 18-25% | Under 18% |
| Real Estate | 28%+ | 20-28% | Under 20% |
| Restaurant | 35%+ | 25-35% | Under 25% |
| Coaching | 35%+ | 25-35% | Under 25% |
| E-commerce | 25%+ | 15-25% | Under 15% |

## Step 6 — The Re-Engagement Goldmine

This is the most overlooked revenue opportunity in every audit. Almost every established business has a dormant list they never contact — and it's often their fastest path to booked calls.

**The hidden list question:**
> "How many email addresses do you have total — across your email inbox, old inquiry forms, past client records, business cards, anything?"

Most business owners underestimate. When they add it up: past clients, old leads, event contacts, referrals, social DMs — the number is usually 200-2,000+ contacts they've never systematically emailed.

**The re-engagement math:**
- 500 dormant contacts
- 20% open rate (they already know the business)
- 3% reply/click rate = 15 conversations restarted
- 20% of those book a call = 3 calls
- 30% close rate = 1 new client
- At $2,500/mo retainer = **$2,500 in month one from one email**

**The immediate win pitch:**
> "Before we even build a new lead gen system — you already have a list. We send one re-engagement email to those contacts next week, and I'd bet you get 2-3 conversations out of it. That's your fastest ROI."

**Re-engagement email formula:**
```
Subject: "Been a while — quick question"

Hey [First Name],

It's been a while since we connected. I'm not going to pretend 
otherwise with some fake "checking in" email.

Here's the real thing: we've been building something new at 
[Business Name] and I genuinely wanted to reach out to people 
who already know us before we open it up publicly.

[One sentence about what's new / what you're offering now]

If you've been thinking about [solving their problem] — 
now's the best time. Reply to this email and let's talk.

[Name]
```

## Step 7 — The Revenue Math (The Closer)

This is what turns the audit from "interesting" to "I need this now."

**Calculate the Leaky Bucket:**

```
Monthly website visitors (estimate):          _______
× Industry avg capture rate w/ lead magnet:   × 3%
= Monthly leads that COULD be captured:       _______

Currently captured per month (estimate):      _______
= Leads being lost monthly:                   _______

× Lead-to-client conversion rate (10%):       × 10%
= Clients being lost monthly:                 _______

× Average client value:                       × $_______
= Monthly revenue leaking from no capture:    $_______/mo
= Annual revenue leaking:                     $_______/yr
```

**Example for a med spa:**
```
Monthly website visitors:          800
× 3% capture rate:                 × 3%
= Capturable leads/month:          24

Currently captured:                ~0
= Lost leads/month:                24

× 10% conversion:                  × 10%
= Lost clients/month:              2.4

× $800 avg client value:           × $800
= Monthly revenue leaking:         $1,920/mo
= Annual revenue leaking:          $23,040/yr
```

Present this in the report and say:
> "A $1,500/month retainer that recovers $23,000/year in leaking revenue has a 15x ROI. That math works on month one."

After presenting the lead gen section:

> "Every month without a capture system, you're paying to get people to your website and Instagram — through your time, your content, your referrals — and then letting them walk out the door with no way to follow up. You've essentially been running a restaurant where people walk in, look at the menu, and leave — and you have no idea who they were or how to reach them again. We fix that."

> "You already did the hard part — you built a list. The money is sitting in your inbox and you haven't sent the email yet. That's the fastest win we could give you in month one."

**If they have no list at all:**
> "The best time to start an email list was two years ago. The second best time is today. Every month you wait, you're adding to the pile of leads you'll never be able to re-contact."
