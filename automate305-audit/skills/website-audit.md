# Skill: Website Audit

## Purpose
Systematically audit a prospect's website and produce scored findings for the report.

## Input Needed
- Website URL
- Business name and industry

## How to Run This Audit

### Step 1 — First Impression Test (5-second rule)
Open the homepage. Without scrolling, answer:
- What does this business do?
- Who is it for?
- What's the next step?

If you can't answer all three in 5 seconds: **score deducted on clarity**

### Step 2 — Above-the-Fold Analysis
Examine only what's visible without scrolling:
- Headline: Is it benefit-driven or just a name/tagline?
- Subheadline: Does it add specificity?
- CTA button: Does it exist? What does it say? Is it prominent?
- Hero image/video: Does it support the message or distract?
- Trust signals: Any logos, reviews, credentials visible?

**Capture:** Quote the actual headline. If it's weak, note why.

### Step 3 — Full Page Scroll
Scroll the entire homepage and note:
- How many CTAs are there? (Ideal: 3-5, all pointing to same action)
- Where is the social proof? (Testimonials, case studies, before/afters)
- Is the offer/pricing mentioned or hidden?
- Is there a lead capture (email opt-in, contact form)?
- Footer: Phone number, address, social links?

### Step 4 — Mobile Check
Open the site on mobile (or use browser dev tools):
- Does it load fast? (Estimate: quick/slow/very slow)
- Is text readable without pinching?
- Do buttons work? Are they tap-friendly?
- Does the main CTA appear above the fold on mobile?

### Step 5 — Copy Quality Check
Read 3 sections of the page copy and note:
- Is it written about the customer (you/your) or the company (we/our)?
- Is it specific (numbers, results, guarantees) or vague (amazing, best, professional)?
- Is there a clear unique value proposition?

### Step 6 — Additional Pages (if relevant)
Check 1-2 other pages:
- Services/Pricing page: Is it clear what they offer and at what price?
- About page: Does it build trust and humanize the brand?
- Contact/Book page: Is it easy? How many steps?

## Common Findings by Industry

**Med Spas / Beauty:**
- Typical gap: Beautiful photos but no CTA. "Book now" buried. No before/after proof above fold.
- Fix: Lead with transformation, add "Book Free Consultation" as primary CTA

**Law Firms:**
- Typical gap: "We fight for you" generic copy. No differentiation. No testimonials.
- Fix: Specific case results, direct CTA, address their fear directly

**Real Estate Agents:**
- Typical gap: All about the agent, not the client. No lead magnet.
- Fix: Buyer/seller guides as lead magnet, client success stories front and center

**Restaurants:**
- Typical gap: Phone number and menu only. No email capture. No story.
- Fix: Reservation widget, loyalty program opt-in, story of the restaurant

**Coaches/Consultants:**
- Typical gap: Vague transformation promises ("live your best life"). No proof. No clear offer.
- Fix: Specific outcomes ("My clients add $X in Y months"), case studies, clear program page

**E-commerce:**
- Typical gap: No email pop-up, no lead magnet, product photos only.
- Fix: Opt-in incentive (10% off), email sequence for abandonment, social proof on product pages
